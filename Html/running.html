  1. 5k

    • 多What is a 5K run?

      • For new runners who want to train to complete a race, 5K competitions are an ideal starting point.
      They are races with a length of 5 kilometers where each runner can give their best, in a relatively
      short time and distance. There are many 5k events, ranging from popular races to highly competitive
      races for the most experienced. These events are very popular with both new and experienced runners,
      and are a great way to raise money for a good cause.

  2. 10k

    • 多What is a 10K race?

      • A 10k race has a distance of 10 kilometers. It is ideal for experienced runners looking for more of a
      challenge. It's the second most popular race after the half marathon and requires a level of fitness
      that balances strength, energy, and stamina. If you've already run a 5K and like to run longer distances,
      the 10K may be the ideal next step. Completing a 10km race is an achievement in itself, being able to be
      happy with your time no matter what. However, it is normal that you want to share with other runners, as
      well as with your previous best times. Your age, cardiovascular condition, and musculoskeletal health can
      influence your individual performance, but the average 10K time is 50 to 70 minutes.



  3. 21k

    • 多Do you know what a half marathon or 21K?

      • A half marathon, or 21K race, is a race that is exactly 21.0975 km long, half a marathon or 42K, which is
      42.195 km. In the grandest scheme of all races, the half marathon is in the middle of the road for racing.



  4. 42k

    • 多Do you know what a 42K marathon?

      • A marathon is a race with a distance of 42.195 kilometers. Although marathons differ in their terrain and
      degree of difficulty, the distance is always 42.195 km. Legend has it that Pheidippides, a Greek messenger,
      ran 40 kilometers from Marathon to Athens to deliver the news of the Athenians' victory over the Persians in
      490 BC. C. He arrived to declare "Nikomen", which is "We won" in Greek. The word is derived from the name of
      the goddess Nike (Victory). Today, you can run a marathon wearing Nike on your feet.
